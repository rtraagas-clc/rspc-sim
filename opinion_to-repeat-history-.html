To Repeat History

At the behest of strongman politicians inundated with her fierce and unwavering campaign to curtail corruption and injustice, former Justice Secretary and Senator Leila De Lima and the protracted handling of her criminal charges only now bearing fruit would augur an approaching future where vested political interests prevail over the sanctity of the rule of law.

Subjected to contempt and scorn by her contemporaries within legislature, progress on De Lima’s cases was virtually suspended under the Duterte presidency within the purview of six judges who either recused themselves from the case or retired. The former head chairperson of the Commission on Human Rights (CHR) was a primary proponent of unveiling the human rights violation of the Davao Death Squad (DDS) during Duterte’s term as mayor of Davao and a vocal critic of his authoritarian regime. After handling the Maguindanao massacre case and conducting raids on the Bilibid prison that uncovered the depths of its illegal activity, she would soon be marked as an enemy for politicians wishing to maintain current power imbalances through any means, those that De Lima proudly fought against.

Cartoon by: Matthew James Mesia

Spending her time of six years detained for alleged disbursements of money from the illegal drug trade at the very Bilibid Prison she sought to reveal, De Lima has borne the brunt of an ineffective and backlogged justice system. Acquitted on the basis of insufficient evidence and plausible deniability, the charges brought against her under the Duterte administration relied on fabricated testimonies, retracted statements, and bogus claims. Despite this, the criminal culpability of the puppeteers behind her conviction is yet to be answered while De Lima awaits the decision on a pending case against her on similar charges despite the lack of admissible evidence.

Without a witness coming through with evidence of false testimonies made under duress during the hearings of her cases and abjured testimonies that pinned De Lima as a recipient of dirty money, Camp Krame would become more of a home for the former Senator than the justice system she sought to reform. It was only towards the end of the Duterte fiat that movement within her case proved favorable in her suit.

Recognized by merits as an integrous public servant and not on the basis of political connections, De Lima’s term as CHR head and Justice Secretary galvanized the country to garner its highest anti-corruption score in Transparency International’s Corruption Perception Index. However, she faces the same fate many activists and citizens with a sense of obligation to give back to their country tagged as perpetrators of state felonies with no recourse to a just, impartial, and unbiased judicial system.

Although justice may have been served with the acquittal of her first case out of a plethora of similar charges, the lack of safeguards and integrity within the system that is tasked to check abuses of power betrays a future destined to repeat history.
