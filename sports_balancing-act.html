Balancing Act

Filipina karateka Jamie Lim reunites with the SEA Games gold medal after defeating Cambodia’s Chakriya Vann, 3-1, at the under 61kg women’s kumite in the 32nd Southeast Asian Games in Cambodia held last week, May 7, 2023. This would mark as her first gold medal finish since her 4 year hiatus from karate.

Lim was on a revenge tour throughout the tournament, first besting Vietnam’s Thi Ngoan Nguyen, who had eliminated her in the previous SEA games, 5-0. Jamie also dominated Mayalsia’s Siti Nur Azwani Nor, 6-3, en route to her championship duel with Chakriya, with the goal of redeeming her bronze finish last year.

What many people do not know, outside Lim’s excellence on the floor, is that she had recently graduated summa cum laude in BS Mathematics at the University of the Philippines. During these years, Lim had taken a four-year hiatus from the sport despite having won a gold medal in the 2019 Manila SEA Games championship.

And now, she is taking her masters in Business Analytics at the Imperial College London, a consensus top 5 university in the world. Lim is aware about the importance of her education and her sport, and is currently trying to balance both without giving up either.

“I cry a lot. It’s very difficult. It’s been so stressful, but it’s so worth it now,” Lim remarked with regards to her balancing act with studies and karate. And despite being committed to the Imperial, Lim did not want to pass up the opportunity to compete in the Cambodia SEA Games, considering her disappointing bronze medal finish in the Vietnam SEA Games.

“I wasn’t supposed to compete here but I decided this year to give it a go because I didn’t want to regret it,” she stated. “I wanted to play. It’s a dream to represent the country. It’s my third SEA Games and the feeling is just as amazing as the very first,” she continued.

As part of her balancing act, Lim will be reporting back to the Imperial after missing three weeks of classes. As a final promise, Lim said that “It’s worth it. I’ll make it work.”
