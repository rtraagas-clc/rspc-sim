The Fall-acy of Discourse

“Joeneeel…”

Sensationalized into a meme that circulated on the nation’s social media platforms, the House of Representatives probe on the alleged drug case of former Senator Leila De Lima and her aide Joenel Sanchez highlighted an often abused argument that continues to divert meaningful discussions into fallacy-ridden discourse, particularly for women who are shamed their sexual activity.

Although the lauded, now ridiculed, public servant has been recently acquitted in one of two drug cases, her image as the former chairperson for the Commission on Human Rights (CHR) and Secretary of the Department of Justice responsible for the lowest Corruption Perception Index in Philippine history has been replaced with a vagrant internet laughing-stock that had no semblance of a connection to the drug trade cases.

At the congressional inquiry, the House allowed the narration of her alleged sex video with Ronnie Dayan, her alleged former driver and lover. Rather than questioning her connection to the complex web of drug trade within the Bilibid Prison, they probed for a deeper connection between De Lima and her Dayan. Rightfully so, she repudiated the decision, questioning their choice on whether they wanted to humiliate her.

Despite a senate resolution disavowing the showing of De Lima’s alleged sex video, recognizing it as “violative of inter-parliamentary courtesy,” it was former President Duterte who raised the video onto the public periphery. The resolution was met with opposition by former Speaker Pantaleon Alvarez, a party mate of the President.

The events of the congressional hearing and the inappropriate place De Lima found herself in are a characterization of more widespread issues of disregarding the merits of women due to sexual activity. Ad hominem attacks have burgeoned and become mainstream within the perverse political atmosphere, shunning impartiality for women like De Lima in a conservative society of the nation.

Although due diligence requires a lengthy check into the background of alleged perpetrators, examining the private lives of public figures with no connection to the topic at hand signals the coming fall-acy of discourse.
