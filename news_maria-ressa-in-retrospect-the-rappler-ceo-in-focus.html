Maria Ressa in retrospect: The Rappler CEO in focus.

As suspects of the Degamo murder begin to clam up, the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) plans to file complaints against Rep. Alfonso Teves Jr. on Wednesday, May 17.

According to the Department of Justice (DOJ) Secretary Crispin Remulla, the six suspects who’ve previously surrendered are now ‘lawyered up’ and are expected to draw out their initial sworn affidavits. “There’s a conspiracy and there’s probably people with a lot of money operating within the conspiracy to be able to afford the lawyers they are now getting,” shared Remulla.

The NBI has stated that they have revisited previous records to solidify the case against Rep. Teves Jr. The DOJ Secretary also said that they’ve acquired information about his whereabouts, saying that Teves has fled to South Korea.

“That will not stop us from filing the proper cases because the statements have been given much earlier with CCTV and cameras in front of witnesses. We suspect that they will change their statements later on and may make recantations of sorts,” Remulla said.

In line with this, Teves has only two-weeks to return to the country after the case is filed before it’s immediately raised to the court.
